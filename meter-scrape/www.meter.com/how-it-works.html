Meter delivers high-performance networks end-to-end

We believe people that build hardware and software should be the ones responsible for it.

From ISP management, routing, switching, wireless, security, DNS security, VPN, SD-WAN, and cell coverage — everything is built for the modern enterprise and delivered as-a-service.

1

ISP procurement & installation

Meter will work with your current ISP or find the best providers to meet your bandwidth needs.

We’ll negotiate rates on your behalf, handle all ISP customer support, and consolidate your billing for a smooth installation. We are ISP agnostic and partner with every local ISP.

Get quotes
2

Hardware provisioning

We design and manufacture our hardware in-house, giving us absolute control over the device firmware, software, and delivery times.

If you already own networking hardware, we’ll buy it back from you and ensure it is recycled responsibly.

3

Custom network design

Our experts design your network unique to your needs.

No two warehouses, schools, or office spaces are the same, and our experts plan for network resiliency and high-performance coverage across every inch of your space.

4

Hardware & cabling installation

Meter installs networks in all commercial spaces.

Full IT closet/server room buildout (with high availability, redundant ISP connections and an uninterruptible power supply), low-voltage cabling, and as many access points as necessary. If you move or expand into a new space, Meter will grow with you.

Meter Installer App
5

Configuration & testing

Meter’s hardware is pre-configured for your unique network via our digital twin capability.

Once our hardware is installed, we can set it up and validate it the same day. For a network cut-over, your old equipment will be left in place until this step is completed.

Network automation

Meter networks are automated, software-managed, and don’t require manual configuration. 

Our software adjusts configurations in real-time or, using the Meter Dashboard, enables you to have deep visibility and control. If an access point goes out, the system is immediately alerted to increase the strength of a neighboring access point.
